ÒHe had left his church school at Ilfracombe at the age of 11, with excellent handwriting and spelling, a good command of English, a well ordered and neat way of keeping his accounts and other records, a knowledge of geography and a rather sketchier grasp of history. Like the Duke of Wellington, he was never completely at ease with Ôall those damned dotsÕ, popularly regarded as essential to the decimal system.
