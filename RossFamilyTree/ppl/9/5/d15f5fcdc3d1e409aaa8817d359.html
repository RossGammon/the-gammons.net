William D. Cutcliffe (Bill) was the first son of Wendell Warner Cutcliffe and Margaret Ellen Pierce. Bill was born on December 16, 1937 in Minneapolis, Minneasota.
Bill's education included the following: Elementary School: Fern Hill, St. Louis Park, Minneasota High School: St. Louis Park High School, St. Louis Park, Minneasota College: University of Minneasota, BA in Business Administration, Advertising Minor, 1960. Memberships: Hawaii Chamber of Commerce, Exchange Club of Honolulu, Hawaii, Hotel and Restaurant Association, Aloha Week Committee, First Presbyterian Church, Big Brothers and Sisters of Hawaii, Inc., Y.M.C.A. of hawaii, University of Minnesota Alumni Association, Sigma Alpha Epsilon Fraternity.
Positions Held: Chief, Unaccompanied Housing, Schofield Barracks, 1989-retirement 1/1/98 Housing Project Manager, US Army, Schofield Barracks, 1979-1989 Logistics Manager, Hale Koa Hotel, Fort DeRussy, Honolulu, 1978-1979 Kapiolani Community College, Food Management Instructor, 1978-1979 Food and Beverage Manager, Hale Koa Hotel 1975-1978 Club Management Specialist, US Army Culbs Pacific Region 1974-1975 Management Consultant, Paradise Development Co. (Makaha Resorts) 1971-1974 General Manager,Waikiki Gateway Hotel (Hyatt Corp.), Honolulu, 1969-1971 Assistant Manager, Ilikai Complex, Honolulu, 1966-1969 Assistant Sales Manager, Leamington Hotel, Minneapolis, 1964-1965 Sales Representative, Sheraton-Ritz Hotel, Minneapolis, 1964-1965 Food and Beverage Manager, Plum Brook Country Club, Sandusky, Ohio 1961-1963 Reservation Manager, Glacier National park Resorts, Montana, 1956-1960 Professional Skater with the Ice Capades out of Minneapolis, Minnesota, 1954-1955.
"William Drummond Cutcliffe was born in Minneapolis, Minnesota, in 1937, where he attended St. Louis Park High School, and then went on to attend the University of Minnesota, where he graduated, majoring in Social Psychology. He also holds a University of Washington Hotel Management Seminar Certificate. His past and present social, fraternal, civic and professional memberships number an even dozen. In his capacity as a hotel operations and management specialist, Bill has traveled extensively, studing hotel managemetn throughout the U.S. and in 17 foreign countries, and has lectured at the University of Hawaii Undergraduate Hotel College. Bill is known for his meticulous manner and dress, and likes to spend money on clothes - - at one time, his wardrobe included 20 suits, each with appropriate matching accessories. By nature, he is inclined to be a "plumger", purchasing a $50,000 apartment home in Honolulu and closing the deal with very little cash. This quality of aggressiveness extends into his work, and is coupled with a creative imagination put to proctical uses. Perhaps one of William's most cherished honors is an award issued to him by the Vice-President of the United States, citing him for his civic work in Minneapolis.Ó
