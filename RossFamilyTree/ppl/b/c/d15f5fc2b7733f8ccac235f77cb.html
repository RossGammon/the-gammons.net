Narrative

Marriage Certificate: Charles, Plymouth: By License, both Full Age. He, a Bachelor, Seaman on board H.M.S. Encounter. She, Mary Ann Earl, a Widow, living at 18 Clarence Street, she made her mark (X), her father John Light, a Hatter. In the presence of R. Owens and Sarah Dowdner. Census of 1851: Bridford, Honestone Lane: with parents George and Ann, (His Mother Sally Holland had died in1836 and George, his Father had remarried), and brothers and sisters. Age 16, a Tailor's Apprentice, born Barnstaple.
Census of 1881: Plymouth, St. Andrews: John Cutcliffe, head, 46, Occupation R.N. Hospital, born Barnstaple. Mary A., 47, wife, born Barnstaple. Agnes M., 5 daughter, Born Plymouth. Frederick, 9, son, born Barnstaple. Kitty L., 3, daughter, born Greenock.
Census of 1891: Plyouth, St. Andrews: His wife and three children are staying with her sister. There is some confusion as no head is shown for their group of three rooms. Her sister is given as sister-in- law to the head, age 71, single May have been born in 1839.
