Narrative

Copywrighted " Critical Path Method". All of family perished in an airplane accident in 1963. Last known address #6 Westford Street, Allston, Ma.. Burried in lot 194, grave 10. No street signs in cemetery. Plaque in the ground facing path. Nearest headstone, St. George, located just behind plaque. Through gate third path on right. Headstone that the path is Larkin about half way down path you see St. Grorge headstone. Plaque reads Lloyd, Elaine and David Cutcliffe.
"John Lloyd Cutcliffe, the first son of Frank and Winifred attended Wales College, Charlottetown; Mount Attison University and Nova Scotia Technical College. He won the Goverrnor Generals Medal for leading his class from Technical College, Halifax. He attended Massachusetts Institute Of Technology and received his Masters Degree in Civil Engineering. He taught at M.I.T., then continued his studies towards his PhD. He became interested in critical path method of planning, scheduling and controlling construction. At M.I.T. he supervised a total of five theses in this area. he lectured widely in the U.S.A. on this phase of work and had several publications. This intelligent, devoted family man, and young wife and infant son met tragedy and untimely deaths when all three perished in a plane accident in 1963."
